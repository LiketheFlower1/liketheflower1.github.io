Here's what creates a better world: More makers. More writers. More artists. A sketchbook on the kitchen table. An essay scribbled in the margins of work notes. The first draft of the memoir you've been dreaming about.

Create because it's fun. Create because it connects you to people. Create because perfectionism sucks. Create because we're drowning in algorithms, and this is how we fight back. 

I'm Rose. I make things -- mostly drawings, custom sketchbooks, and letters to my poet friend -- but sometimes the ADHD takes over, and I needlefelt too many toadstools. I'm in recovery from an affair with liquor, a habit of skipping meals, and the crippling belief that I could only make art if it was good. Today, I live on a farm in Pennsylvania with a rotating cast of rescue animals who have never once questioned my creative output.
