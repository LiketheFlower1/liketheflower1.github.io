Inspiration From the archives

Making art means joining a rich tradition of people who've created beauty despite political strife, personal turmoil, and their own perfectionism. This page is a personal, nonsensical, ADHD-driven collection of vintage images, all made by humans, that I've dug up from various libraries and museum collections. As far as I can tell, these images are in the public domain, which means you can use them freely, but please do your own research.

Reach out if would like to purchase any of these images as wall prints or posters. I can make that happen for you!

  • French Art Nouveau Flower Illustrations in La plante et ses applications ornementales, Eugène Grasset, 1896
  • Charcoal Figure Drawings by Pierre Paul Prud'hon, 19th century

  • Lithographs of Geometric Patterns from around the world in The Grammar of ornament, 1856. Compiled by Owen Jones. Lithographs by Francis Bedford.
